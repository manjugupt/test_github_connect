Sudha Murty
Indian writer and philanthropist(born 1915)

Sudha Murty began her professional career in computer science and engineering. She is the chairperson of the Infosys Foundation and a member of the public health care initiatives of the Gates Foundation.

About the Indian writer and philanthropist

Sudha Murty was born into a Kannada-Speaking Deshastha Madhav Brahmin family on 19 August 1915 in Shiggaon, Haveri in Karanataka, India, the daughter of R.H. Kulkarni, a surgeon, and his wife Vimala Kulkarni, a school teacher.


Career:-

Sudha Murty become the first female engineer hired at India's largest auto manufacture TATA Engineering and Locomotive Company(TELCO). She joined the company as a Development Engineer in Pune and then worked in Mumbai & jameshedpur as well. She had written a postcard to the company's chairman complaining of the "men only" gender boas at TELCO. As a result, she was granted a special interview and hired immediately. She later joined Walchand Group of Industries at Pune as Senior System Analyst.
sudha Murty has written and published many books which include novels, non-fication, travelogues, technical books, and memoirs. Her books have been tanslated into all mojor Indian Languages. She is also a columnist for English and Kannads newspaper.

  • Sudha Murty married N.R. Narayan Murthy while emplyed as an engineer at TELCO in Pune. The couple have two children, including fashion designer Akashta Murty, who is married to the current British Prime Minister, Rishi Sunak.

    Her siblings include Caltech astrophysicist Shrinivas Kulkarni and Jaishree Deshpande(Wife of Gururaj Deshpande)who co-founded the Deshpande Center for Technological Innovation at MIT.
